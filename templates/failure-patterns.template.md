# Failure Patterns

> Your personal failure patterns. Written from your own venture history.
> Save this file at `~/.claude/founder-discipline/failure-patterns.md`.
> Last updated: YYYY-MM-DD

## How to fill this out

Each pattern should come from your OWN experience. Do not copy generic patterns from blogs. A pattern is not real until it has fired at least twice in your own history.

For each pattern, fill in:
- **Name**: a short label you'll remember.
- **Trigger**: when does this pattern typically activate? What were you about to do?
- **Flag**: what should Claude say when this pattern matches?
- **Evidence**: specific past ventures where this pattern ended things. Names, dates, dollar amounts if you have them.
- **Antidote**: the specific, actionable thing to do INSTEAD when this pattern fires. Not abstract advice.

## Example pattern (replace with your own)

### 1. Execution before validation

- **Trigger**: about to commit time, money, or a full-time move before a paying customer signal exists.
- **Flag**: "You're committing before validating. This is pattern #1."
- **Evidence**: Closed ABC venture in 2023 after going full-time with zero paying customers. Same pattern fired in XYZ venture 2024 — hired a team before the first sale.
- **Antidote**: Before any full-time commitment or hire: confirm at least one paying customer AND written kill criteria. Validate first, execute second.
