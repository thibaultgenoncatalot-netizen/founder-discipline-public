# Business Criteria

> Personal weighted criteria for evaluating business ideas.
> Save this file at `~/.claude/founder-discipline/business-criteria.md`.
> Last updated: YYYY-MM-DD

## How to fill this out

These are YOUR criteria. Not universal best practices. What matters to YOU right now, given your circumstances, runway, skills, and goals.

Each criterion has a weight: 3x (must-have), 2x (strongly prefer), or 1x (nice-to-have). Custom weights are allowed but rare.

Aim for 8-12 weighted criteria. More than 15 dilutes the signal. Fewer than 6 misses important dimensions.

## Hard elimination criteria (binary NOs)

Any idea violating one of these is rejected before weighted scoring. No elaborate analysis — out.

- [e.g., Requires cold enterprise sales]
- [e.g., Requires $100K+ upfront capital]
- [e.g., Requires deep technical skills I don't have and don't want to learn]
- [e.g., Requires 52-week content consistency before monetization]
- [e.g., Requires local employment in a country I can't work in]

Keep this list to 3-7 items. More than 7 usually means over-reactive filtering.

## Notes

- Review every 6 months, or after any venture kill. Priorities change.
- If a criterion consistently scores 5/5 across all ideas, it's not discriminating — reweight or drop.
- If a criterion has never shifted the verdict in a real comparison, it may be cosmetic.
